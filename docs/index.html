DATA606 Presentation

Paycheck Protection Program and the SBA

Author

Ana Collado

Abstract

We examined the question Is there a significant difference in average loan forgiveness proportion for Hubzone Businesses vs non Hubzone businesses? With a P value at 0.182 at the alpha level of 0.05– we are 95% confident that the businesses who are part of a HubZone have an average difference in the proportion forgiven of their PPP Loan between -0.00452 and 0.00083. The interval includes zero, suggesting there is not a significant difference in proportion of loan forgiven. We fail to reject the null hypothesis.

The lack of significance in the loan forgiveness proportion between the groups (one of which is established as needing extra support) suggests the HubZone program might require reevaluation!

• A long-winded Introduction

In the aftermath of COVID, businesses in need of help applied for SBA backed loans under the Paycheck Protection Program. 1 The loans were intended to be forgiven if the business met specific criteria.2 The Small Business Administration, with the assistance of The Office of Inspector General, released a report in 2023 informing the public of $200 billion dollars disbursed to fraudulent claims.3

The SBA released a public list of all business entities that applied and received PPP loan funds.4 The dataset is a robust list of business of varying sizes, ages, and sectors across the United States (with over 10 million data points). In Part A of this exploration we establish that the data alludes to, at the very least, lax expectations and soft requirements for SBA backing– which also suggests the original hypothesis that the SBA’s handling of things made it more convenient to commit fraud.

Statistical exploration could suggest that approval requirements were less rigid were due to business’ indicator status such as “HubZone” (answered Yes or No) for businesses in locations that experience economic hardship). Out of the abundance of questions to persue, well examine the question Is there a significant difference in average loan forgiveness proportion for Hubzone Businesses? As a bonus we’ll look at How much more loans (in dollar value) were approved for businesses marked “Yes” in Hubzone category? Well also take a look at the LMI Indicator in comparison. (Lower to Medium Income)

• Disclosures & Limitations

The Data:

In the data, loans were disbursed to many claims that did not fill the forms completely. We might not trust that a business is truly in a Hubzone when their Employer Identification Numbers (an identifier which any registered business with employees should have) is not avaiable in the dataset. Many items were self reported with little fact check. We examine all of the inconsistencies in Part A of this project. but in connection to our study here, while we might examine the validity of hubzone response, the dollar amounts are valid, and were disbursed. Additionally there are observations exempt whose information is protected by law (whose verbiage by the way is incredibly broad).

The Work

This project is not finished as it takes took longer than I had imagined to examine 11 million data points. However, in retrospect, because of the magnitude of observations, the hypothesis would have better fit in a normal distribution had I taken a random sample of the data. It is certainly too late now but its something I did not think of at the time

HubZones

Upon presentation a question was raised about the normalization of the comparison plot exploring the question, How much more loans (in dollar value) were approved for businesses marked “Yes” in Hubzone category? The volume of business in qualifying HubZones vs non should be established for normalization of the report.

Unfortunately there are many incomplete observations that would help with addresses. Further, the SBA does not offer a list of zipcodes to cross reference for a count comparison of the size of each group.

While this might be a deadend or perhaps an extensive project to take on, it would be a little more simple to examine how the Hubzone designations are established. A business must check to see if they are in a HubZone area and submit an application to be identified as such. Its perfectly possible businesses in the dataset are not identified as Hubzone despite qualifying.

There is no significant difference in the average proportion of loan forgiveness between the Hubzone businesses and nonHubzone businesses groups.

H1 Alternative Hypothesis:

There is a significant difference in the average proportion of loan forgiveness between the Hubzone businesses and NonHubzone businesses groups.

Note

The P value is 0.182 at the alpha level of 0.05. I am 95% confident that the businesses who are part of a HubZone have a difference in the proportion forgiven of their PPP Loan of about -0.00452 and 0.00083. The interval does include zero marking the difference as not significant. So, we fail to reject the null hypothesis!

• Conclusion

Although we found there is no significant difference in average PPP Loan forgiveness proportion between Hubzone and non Hubzone businesses, it seems like a separate issue might need some investigation. The Hubzone program is meant for businesses that need the extra assistance because of recognized issues within their location. This exploration suggests we should reexamine the program.
